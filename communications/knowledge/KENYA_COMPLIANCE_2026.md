## 14-Day Rule (Event-Based Compliance)

### What is the 14-Day Rule?

Starting 2026, all company changes must be filed with BRS Kenya **within 14 days** of occurrence.

### Events Covered

#### 1. Director Changes
**Appointment of Director (CR2)**:
- File within 14 days of appointment
- Fee: KES 650
- Required information: Full name, ID/Passport, KRA PIN, address, appointment date
- Penalty: Automatic fine after 14 days

**Resignation/Removal of Director (CR8)**:
- File within 14 days of resignation/removal
- Fee: KES 650
- Required: Resignation letter or board resolution, effective date
- Penalty: Automatic fine after 14 days

#### 2. Address Changes
**Change of Registered Office (CR15)**:
- File within 14 days of address change
- Fee: KES 650
- Required: New address, proof of address, board resolution
- Penalty: Automatic fine after 14 days

#### 3. Beneficial Ownership Changes
**Update Beneficial Ownership (BOF1)**:
- File within 30 days (not 14 days) of change
- No fee
- Required when: Someone acquires/disposes 10%+ ownership
- Penalty: KES 500,000 for non-compliance

#### 4. Share Transfers
**Transfer of Shares (CR9)**:
- File within 30 days (not 14 days) of transfer
- Fee: KES 650
- Required: Share transfer deed, transferor/transferee details
- Penalty: Late filing fine

### Penalties for Non-Compliance

- **Days 1-14**: No penalty (compliance window)
- **Day 15+**: Automatic penalty applied
- **Penalty amount**: To be gazetted by Registrar
- **Consequences**: Cannot obtain CR12, company marked non-compliant

### Best Practices

1. **Immediate Documentation**: Document all changes immediately when they occur
2. **Set Reminders**: Use calendar alerts for 7 days and 13 days after change
3. **Batch Processing**: If multiple changes, file all within 14 days
4. **Digital Filing**: Use BRS Kenya portal for instant submission
5. **Confirmation**: Always download filing confirmation receipt

## Annual Compliance Calendar

### Annual Returns (CR29)

**Due Date**: On or before incorporation anniversary

**Fee**: KES 1,000

**Penalty**: KES 500 per year of delay

**Example Timeline**:
- Company incorporated: 15th March 2023
- First CR29 due: 15th March 2024
- Second CR29 due: 15th March 2025
- Third CR29 due: 15th March 2026

**Information Required**:
- Current directors and details
- Current shareholders and shareholding
- Share capital structure
- Registered office address
- Company secretary (if appointed)
- Confirmation of company status

**Filing Process**:
1. Log in to BRS Kenya 30 days before due date
2. Review pre-filled information
3. Update any changes
4. Verify accuracy
5. Pay KES 1,000
6. Submit and download receipt


### Annual General Meeting (AGM)

**Timing Requirements**:
- **First AGM**: Within 18 months of incorporation
- **Subsequent AGMs**: Within 15 months of previous AGM
- **Financial Statements**: Within 6 months of financial year end

**Example Timeline**:
- Company incorporated: 1st January 2023
- Financial year end: 31st December 2023
- First AGM deadline: 30th June 2024 (18 months)
- Financial statements due: 30th June 2024 (6 months after FYE)
- Second AGM deadline: 30th September 2025 (15 months after first AGM)

**AGM Requirements**:
- Notice period: 21 days for public companies, 14 days for private companies
- Quorum: As per Articles of Association (typically 2 members)
- Agenda: Approve financial statements, elect directors, appoint auditors
- Minutes: Must be recorded and filed

**Virtual AGMs** (2026 Update):
- Fully permitted under Companies Act
- Must provide video/audio link
- All shareholders must have access
- Record attendance electronically
- Minutes must note virtual attendance

### Financial Statements Filing

**Deadline**: Within 6 months of financial year end

**Requirements**:
- Balance sheet
- Income statement
- Cash flow statement
- Notes to accounts
- Directors' report
- Auditor's report (if required)

**Audit Requirements**:
- Mandatory if turnover > KES 5 million
- Optional if turnover < KES 5 million
- Foreign companies: Always required

## Entity-Specific Deadlines

### Private Limited Companies

**Annual Requirements**:
- CR29 (Annual Return): On incorporation anniversary, KES 1,000
- AGM: Within 15 months of previous AGM
- Financial Statements: Within 6 months of FYE
- BOF1: Within 30 days of ownership changes

**Event-Based (14-Day Rule)**:
- Director changes (CR2/CR8): 14 days, KES 650
- Address changes (CR15): 14 days, KES 650
- Share transfers (CR9): 30 days, KES 650

### Limited Liability Partnerships (LLP)

**Annual Requirements**:
- LLP Annual Return: On incorporation anniversary, KES 1,000
- Statement of Accounts: Within 6 months of FYE
- Partner changes: Within 14 days

**Specific Requirements**:
- Minimum 2 designated partners
- Partnership agreement must be filed
- Changes to agreement: File within 14 days

### Business Names

**Registration**: KES 1,050, valid for 5 years

**Renewal**: Before expiry date, KES 1,050

**Changes**:
- Change of proprietor: Within 14 days
- Change of business address: Within 14 days
- Change of business nature: Within 14 days

**Penalties**:
- Operating with expired business name: KES 10,000 fine
- Late renewal: Additional KES 500

### Foreign Companies

**Annual Requirements**:
- Annual Return: On incorporation anniversary, KES 1,000
- Accounts Filing: Within 6 months of FYE (home country accounts)
- List of Directors: Update within 14 days of changes

**Specific Requirements**:
- Notify changes in home country: Within 30 days
- Maintain registered office in Kenya
- Appoint local representative
- File certified copies of home country documents

## Tax Compliance

### KRA PIN Requirements

**Who Needs KRA PIN**:
- All companies (mandatory)
- All directors (mandatory)
- All employees (mandatory)
- All shareholders (recommended)

**How to Obtain**:
1. Visit iTax portal (https://itax.kra.go.ke)
2. Register as new taxpayer
3. Provide ID/Passport and details
4. Submit application
5. Receive PIN via email (instant)

**Company KRA PIN**:
- Applied after company incorporation
- Requires Certificate of Incorporation
- Directors' KRA PINs needed
- Processing: 1-3 days

### PAYE (Pay As You Earn)

**What is PAYE**: Tax on employee salaries

**Filing Deadline**: 9th of following month

**Payment Deadline**: 9th of following month

**Rates** (2026):
- 0 - 24,000: 10%
- 24,001 - 32,333: 25%
- 32,334 and above: 30%

**Monthly Process**:
1. Calculate employee gross pay
2. Deduct PAYE based on tax bands
3. File P9 form on iTax by 9th
4. Pay PAYE to KRA by 9th
5. Issue payslips to employees

**Penalties**:
- Late filing: KES 10,000 or 5% of tax due (whichever is higher)
- Late payment: 5% penalty + 1% interest per month

### SHIF (Social Health Insurance Fund)

**What is SHIF**: Mandatory health insurance (replaced NHIF in 2024)

**Rate**: 2.75% of gross salary

**Filing Deadline**: 9th of following month

**Payment Deadline**: 9th of following month

**Employer Obligations**:
- Deduct 2.75% from employee salary
- Remit to SHIF by 9th
- File monthly returns on SHIF portal
- Maintain employee records

**Penalties**:
- Late payment: 5% penalty + 1% interest per month
- Non-registration: KES 50,000 fine

### NSSF (National Social Security Fund)

**What is NSSF**: Mandatory pension contribution

**Rates** (2026):
- Tier I (0 - 7,000): 6% (3% employee + 3% employer)
- Tier II (7,001 - 36,000): 6% (3% employee + 3% employer)

**Filing Deadline**: 15th of following month

**Payment Deadline**: 15th of following month

**Monthly Process**:
1. Calculate employee pensionable pay
2. Deduct employee contribution (3%)
3. Add employer contribution (3%)
4. File on NSSF portal by 15th
5. Pay total to NSSF by 15th

**Penalties**:
- Late payment: 5% penalty + 2% interest per month
- Non-registration: KES 20,000 fine

### VAT (Value Added Tax)

**Who Must Register**:
- Businesses with turnover > KES 5 million per year
- Voluntary registration available for smaller businesses

**Rate**: 16% (standard rate)

**Filing Deadline**: 20th of following month

**Payment Deadline**: 20th of following month

**Monthly Process**:
1. Calculate output VAT (VAT charged to customers)
2. Calculate input VAT (VAT paid on purchases)
3. Net VAT = Output VAT - Input VAT
4. File VAT return on iTax by 20th
5. Pay net VAT to KRA by 20th

**Zero-Rated Supplies** (0% VAT):
- Exports
- Agricultural supplies
- Medical supplies
- Educational materials

**Exempt Supplies** (No VAT):
- Financial services
- Insurance
- Residential rent
- Unprocessed agricultural products

**Penalties**:
- Late filing: KES 10,000 or 5% of tax due
- Late payment: 5% penalty + 1% interest per month
- Failure to register: 100% of tax due

## eTIMS Requirements

### What is eTIMS?

**eTIMS** (Electronic Tax Invoice Management System) is KRA's real-time invoice transmission system.

**Purpose**:
- Real-time transmission of invoices to KRA
- Combat tax evasion
- Ensure VAT compliance
- Track business transactions

**Mandatory For**:
- All VAT-registered businesses (from 2023)
- All businesses with turnover > KES 5 million (from 2024)
- All government suppliers (mandatory)

### eTIMS Device Requirements

**Options**:
1. **eTIMS-enabled POS device**: Physical device from KRA-approved vendors
2. **eTIMS software**: Desktop/mobile app integrated with KRA
3. **eTIMS API**: For businesses with existing systems

**Cost**:
- POS device: KES 15,000 - 30,000 (one-time)
- Software: Free (KRA-provided) or paid (third-party)
- API integration: Development cost varies

### How eTIMS Works

**Invoice Generation Process**:
1. Create invoice in eTIMS system
2. System generates unique invoice number
3. Invoice transmitted to KRA in real-time
4. KRA validates and approves
5. QR code generated on invoice
6. Customer receives eTIMS-compliant invoice

**Invoice Requirements**:
- Unique invoice number (KRA-generated)
- QR code (for verification)
- Seller's KRA PIN
- Buyer's KRA PIN (if available)
- Date and time
- Items/services description
- VAT breakdown
- Total amount

### Reverse Invoicing Procedures

**What is Reverse Invoicing?**

When a supplier doesn't have eTIMS, the buyer creates an invoice on behalf of the supplier.

**When to Use**:
- Supplier is not eTIMS-registered
- Supplier is small-scale trader
- Supplier is farmer or informal sector
- Emergency purchases

**Step-by-Step Process**:

1. **Verify Supplier Status**:
   - Check if supplier has eTIMS
   - Request supplier's KRA PIN
   - Confirm supplier details

2. **Create Reverse Invoice**:
   - Log in to your eTIMS system
   - Select "Reverse Invoice" option
   - Enter supplier details:
     * Supplier name
     * Supplier KRA PIN (if available)
     * Supplier ID/Passport
   - Enter transaction details:
     * Items/services purchased
     * Quantities
     * Unit prices
     * Total amount

3. **Generate Invoice**:
   - System creates invoice on behalf of supplier
   - Unique invoice number generated
   - QR code created
   - Transmitted to KRA

4. **Payment and Records**:
   - Pay supplier
   - Give supplier copy of reverse invoice
   - Retain copy for your records
   - Invoice valid for expense claim

**Compliance Requirements**:
- Must have supplier's consent (verbal acceptable)
- Supplier details must be accurate
- Transaction must be genuine
- Keep supporting documents (delivery notes, receipts)

**Penalties for Misuse**:
- Creating fake reverse invoices: KES 500,000 fine
- False supplier information: KES 200,000 fine
- Tax evasion via reverse invoicing: 100% of tax + penalties

### "No eTIMS, No Expense" Enforcement

**What Does This Mean?**

Starting 2024, businesses cannot claim tax deductions for expenses without eTIMS invoices.

**Key Rules**:

1. **Expense Deduction Denied**:
   - If invoice doesn't have eTIMS QR code
   - If invoice not transmitted to KRA
   - If invoice from non-compliant supplier

2. **Tax Implications**:
   - Expense not deductible for corporate tax
   - Increases taxable income
   - Higher tax liability

3. **Exceptions**:
   - Reverse invoices (properly created)
   - Exempt supplies (residential rent, etc.)
   - Small purchases < KES 1,000 (with receipt)

**Example**:
- Company buys goods for KES 100,000 without eTIMS invoice
- Cannot claim KES 100,000 as expense
- Taxable income increases by KES 100,000
- Additional tax: KES 30,000 (at 30% corporate tax rate)

**Best Practices**:
- Always request eTIMS invoice
- Verify QR code before payment
- If supplier has no eTIMS, create reverse invoice
- Keep digital copies of all eTIMS invoices
- Reconcile eTIMS invoices with KRA monthly

### "No eTIMS, No Payment" Policy

**What Does This Mean?**

Government and large corporates will not pay suppliers without eTIMS invoices.

**Who Enforces This**:
- All government ministries and departments
- State corporations
- County governments
- Large private companies (banks, telcos, etc.)

**Implementation**:

1. **Procurement Requirements**:
   - Suppliers must be eTIMS-registered
   - Proof of eTIMS compliance required
   - eTIMS certificate mandatory for tenders

2. **Invoice Submission**:
   - Only eTIMS invoices accepted
   - Must have valid QR code
   - Must be verifiable on KRA portal

3. **Payment Processing**:
   - Finance department verifies eTIMS invoice
   - Checks QR code authenticity
   - Confirms transmission to KRA
   - Payment only if compliant

**Consequences for Suppliers**:
- Cannot supply to government
- Cannot participate in tenders
- Payment delayed indefinitely
- Contract termination possible

**How to Comply**:
1. Register for eTIMS on iTax portal
2. Acquire eTIMS device or software
3. Train staff on eTIMS usage
4. Generate eTIMS invoices for all sales
5. Maintain eTIMS compliance

### eTIMS Penalties

| Violation | Penalty (KES) |
|-----------|---------------|
| Operating without eTIMS (if required) | 1,000,000 |
| Issuing non-eTIMS invoice (if registered) | 500,000 |
| Tampering with eTIMS device | 2,000,000 |
| Creating fake eTIMS invoices | 500,000 |
| Failure to transmit invoices | 200,000 |
| Using unapproved eTIMS device | 1,000,000 |

### eTIMS Compliance Checklist

**Daily**:
- [ ] Generate eTIMS invoices for all sales
- [ ] Verify QR codes on invoices
- [ ] Transmit invoices to KRA in real-time
- [ ] Keep digital copies

**Weekly**:
- [ ] Reconcile eTIMS invoices with sales records
- [ ] Check for failed transmissions
- [ ] Retransmit any failed invoices
- [ ] Backup eTIMS data

**Monthly**:
- [ ] Download eTIMS report from iTax
- [ ] Reconcile with VAT return
- [ ] Verify all invoices transmitted
- [ ] Check for KRA notices

**Quarterly**:
- [ ] Review eTIMS compliance status
- [ ] Update eTIMS software/firmware
- [ ] Train staff on any changes
- [ ] Audit eTIMS processes

## AGM Requirements

### Timing Requirements

**First AGM**:
- Within 18 months of incorporation
- Must approve first financial statements

**Subsequent AGMs**:
- Within 15 months of previous AGM
- Annual requirement

**Financial Year End**:
- AGM must be within 6 months of FYE
- To approve financial statements for that year

**Example Timeline**:
- Incorporation: 1st January 2023
- FYE: 31st December 2023
- First AGM deadline: 30th June 2024 (18 months OR 6 months after FYE)
- Second AGM deadline: 30th September 2025 (15 months after first AGM)

### Notice Requirements

**Notice Period**:
- Private companies: 14 days minimum
- Public companies: 21 days minimum
- Can be longer per Articles of Association

**Notice Content**:
- Date, time, and location of AGM
- Agenda items
- Resolutions to be passed
- Financial statements (if approving)
- Proxy form

**How to Send Notice**:
- Email (if Articles allow)
- Registered post
- Hand delivery
- Published in newspaper (for public companies)

### Virtual AGM Provisions (2026)

**Fully Permitted**:
- Companies Act allows virtual AGMs
- No physical attendance required
- Must be in Articles of Association

**Requirements**:
- Video/audio conferencing platform
- All shareholders must have access
- Technical support available
- Record attendance electronically
- Voting mechanism in place

**Hybrid AGMs**:
- Physical and virtual attendance allowed
- Must accommodate both
- Equal participation rights

**Minutes**:
- Must note virtual attendees
- Record technical issues (if any)
- Attach attendance log
- File as usual

### Quorum Rules

**Minimum Quorum**:
- As per Articles of Association
- Typically 2 members present (in person or by proxy)
- If quorum not met, adjourn to another date

**Proxy Voting**:
- Shareholders can appoint proxies
- Proxy form must be submitted before AGM
- Proxy counts towards quorum

### AGM Agenda

**Standard Agenda Items**:
1. Call to order and confirmation of quorum
2. Approval of previous AGM minutes
3. Chairman's report
4. Presentation of financial statements
5. Approval of financial statements
6. Declaration of dividends (if any)
7. Election/re-election of directors
8. Appointment of auditors
9. Any other business
10. Adjournment

**Special Resolutions** (if any):
- Require 75% approval
- Must be in AGM notice
- Examples: Change of name, increase capital, amend Articles

### AGM Minutes

**What to Record**:
- Date, time, location
- Attendees (names and shareholding)
- Apologies
- Quorum confirmation
- Resolutions passed/rejected
- Voting results
- Any objections or dissent
- Adjournment time

**Signing**:
- Chairman signs minutes
- Secretary countersigns
- File in minute book

**Filing**:
- Keep in company records
- Available for inspection
- May be required by Registrar

### Penalties for Non-Compliance

**Failure to Hold AGM**:
- Fine: KES 50,000
- Directors personally liable
- Company may be struck off

**Late AGM**:
- Fine: KES 20,000
- Compliance order from Registrar

**No Financial Statements**:
- Fine: KES 20,000
- Cannot obtain CR12

## Statutory Record Keeping

### Records to Maintain

#### 1. Register of Members (Shareholders)
**Contents**:
- Full name and address
- Number of shares held
- Share class
- Date of becoming member
- Date of ceasing to be member
- Transfer details

**Retention**: Permanent (life of company)

**Location**: Registered office or as notified to Registrar

**Access**: Members can inspect, public can request copies

#### 2. Register of Directors
**Contents**:
- Full name and former names
- Date of birth
- Nationality
- ID/Passport number
- Residential address
- Business occupation
- Date of appointment
- Date of cessation

**Retention**: Permanent

**Location**: Registered office

**Access**: Public can inspect

#### 3. Register of Secretaries
**Contents**:
- Full name
- Address
- Date of appointment
- Date of cessation

**Retention**: Permanent

**Location**: Registered office

**Access**: Public can inspect

#### 4. Register of Beneficial Owners (BO E-Register)
**Contents**:
- Full name
- Date of birth
- Nationality
- ID/Passport number
- Residential address
- Percentage of ownership/control
- Nature of control
- Date of becoming BO

**Retention**: Permanent

**Location**: BRS Kenya portal (electronic)

**Access**: Publicly accessible online

#### 5. Minute Books
**Contents**:
- Board meeting minutes
- Shareholder meeting minutes
- AGM minutes
- Resolutions

**Retention**: 10 years minimum

**Location**: Registered office

**Access**: Members can inspect board minutes, all can see shareholder minutes

#### 6. Accounting Records
**Contents**:
- Financial statements
- Invoices and receipts
- Bank statements
- Payroll records
- Tax returns
- Audit reports

**Retention**: 7 years minimum (10 years recommended)

**Location**: Registered office or as notified

**Access**: Directors and auditors, KRA on request

#### 7. Share Certificates and Transfer Deeds
**Contents**:
- Issued share certificates
- Share transfer forms
- Transfer deeds
- Consideration paid

**Retention**: Permanent

**Location**: Registered office

**Access**: Relevant shareholders

### Digital vs Physical Records

**Digital Records** (2026 Update):
- Fully acceptable under Companies Act
- Must be backed up regularly
- Must be accessible at registered office
- Cloud storage permitted
- Encryption recommended

**Physical Records**:
- Still acceptable
- Must be stored securely
- Fire and water protection
- Access control

**Best Practice**:
- Maintain both digital and physical
- Scan all physical documents
- Cloud backup for digital
- Regular audits of records

### Record Inspection Rights

**Who Can Inspect**:
- **Members**: Can inspect shareholder register, minutes of shareholder meetings
- **Directors**: Can inspect all company records
- **Auditors**: Can inspect all financial records
- **Registrar**: Can request any records
- **KRA**: Can request tax-related records
- **Public**: Can inspect director register, BO register

**How to Request**:
1. Written request to company secretary
2. State purpose of inspection
3. Company has 7 days to respond
4. Inspection at registered office during business hours
5. Can request copies (fee may apply)

### Penalties for Non-Maintenance

**Failure to Maintain Registers**:
- Fine: KES 100,000
- Directors personally liable

**Refusal to Allow Inspection**:
- Fine: KES 50,000
- Court order to compel

**False Records**:
- Fine: KES 1,000,000
- Imprisonment up to 2 years

- **High**: Likely to occur within 6 months
- **Medium**: May occur within 12 months
