## What is BRS Kenya

**BRS Kenya** (Business Registration Service) is the official online portal for company and business registration in Kenya, operated by the Office of the Registrar of Companies.

### Main Services

1. **Companies Registry** - Register and manage companies
2. **MPSR (Movable Property Security Rights)** - Register security interests in movable property
3. **Official Receiver** - Insolvency and bankruptcy services
4. **Hire Purchase** - Register hire purchase agreements

### Portal Access
- **Website**: https://brs.go.ke
- **Support**: support@brs.go.ke
- **Helpline**: 020-2222333

## Entity Registration

### 1. Private Limited Company (Ltd)

**Form**: CR1 (Company Registration Form)

**Requirements**:
- Minimum 1 director (must be natural person)
- Minimum 1 shareholder (can be individual or corporate)
- Company name (check availability first)
- Registered office address in Kenya
- Share capital details
- Directors' details (ID/Passport, KRA PIN, email, phone)
- Shareholders' details

**Fees**:
- Share capital up to KES 100,000: **KES 10,100**
- Share capital KES 100,001 - 1,000,000: **KES 11,100**
- Share capital KES 1,000,001 - 5,000,000: **KES 12,100**
- Share capital above KES 5,000,000: **KES 13,100**

**Processing Time**: 1-3 business days

**Documents Issued**:
- Certificate of Incorporation
- CR12 (Certificate of Registration)
- Memorandum and Articles of Association

### 2. Business Name (Trading As)

**Form**: BN2 (Business Name Registration)

**Requirements**:
- Proprietor details (individual or company)
- Business name (check availability)
- Business address
- Nature of business
- KRA PIN

**Fees**: **KES 1,050**

**Validity**: 5 years (renewable)

**Processing Time**: 1-2 business days

### 3. Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)

**Form**: LLP1 (LLP Registration Form)

**Requirements**:
- Minimum 2 partners (individuals or corporate)
- LLP name (must end with "LLP")
- Registered office in Kenya
- Designated partners (minimum 2)
- Partnership agreement

**Fees**: **KES 10,100**

**Processing Time**: 2-3 business days

### 4. Foreign Company (Branch/Subsidiary)

**Form**: CR19 (Foreign Company Registration)

**Requirements**:
- Certificate of Incorporation from home country
- Memorandum and Articles (certified copies)
- Board resolution to establish in Kenya
- Registered office address in Kenya
- Local representative details
- Notarized and apostilled documents

**Fees**: **KES 20,000**

**Processing Time**: 5-7 business days

**Additional Requirements**:
- Annual filing of accounts (within 6 months of financial year end)
- Notify any changes in home country within 30 days

## Annual Returns (CR29)

### What is CR29?

Annual Return is a mandatory filing that updates the Registrar on company status, directors, shareholders, and share capital.

### Filing Requirements

**Form**: CR29 (Annual Return)

**Deadline**: On or before the anniversary of incorporation date

**Example**: Company incorporated on 15th March 2023 must file CR29 by 15th March 2024

**Fee**: **KES 1,000**

### Information Required

- Current directors and their details
- Current shareholders and shareholding
- Share capital structure
- Registered office address
- Company secretary details (if appointed)
- Confirmation of company status

### Penalties for Late Filing

**Penalty**: **KES 500 per year** of delay

**Examples**:
- 1 year late: KES 1,000 (filing fee) + KES 500 (penalty) = **KES 1,500**
- 2 years late: KES 1,000 + KES 1,000 (penalty) = **KES 2,000**
- 3 years late: KES 1,000 + KES 1,500 (penalty) = **KES 2,500**

### Consequences of Non-Filing

- Company may be struck off the register
- Directors may be personally liable
- Cannot obtain CR12 (Certificate of Good Standing)
- Cannot participate in tenders or government contracts
- Bank accounts may be frozen

### How to File CR29

1. Log in to BRS Kenya portal
2. Select your company
3. Navigate to "Annual Returns"
4. Complete CR29 form online
5. Verify all information
6. Pay KES 1,000 fee via M-Pesa or bank
7. Submit and download receipt

## Beneficial Ownership (BOF1)

### What is Beneficial Ownership?

A beneficial owner is any individual who ultimately owns or controls 10% or more of a company's shares or voting rights, or exercises significant control.

### Legal Requirements

**Form**: BOF1 (Beneficial Ownership Form)

**Threshold**: 10% or more ownership/control

**Deadline**: Within 30 days of:
- Company incorporation
- Change in beneficial ownership
- Acquiring 10% or more stake

### Who Must Be Declared?

- Individuals owning 10%+ shares
- Individuals with 10%+ voting rights
- Individuals with power to appoint/remove directors
- Individuals exercising significant influence

### Information Required

- Full name and ID/Passport number
- Date of birth and nationality
- Residential address
- KRA PIN
- Percentage of ownership/control
- Nature of control (shares, voting rights, other)
- Date of becoming beneficial owner

### Penalties

**Non-compliance penalty**: **KES 500,000** or imprisonment up to 2 years, or both

### How to File BOF1

1. Log in to BRS Kenya portal
2. Select your company
3. Navigate to "Beneficial Ownership"
4. Click "Add Beneficial Owner"
5. Complete BOF1 form with owner details
6. Submit (no fee for BOF1 filing)
7. Download confirmation

### BO E-Register

All companies must maintain a Beneficial Ownership E-Register accessible through BRS portal. This register is:
- Publicly accessible
- Updated within 30 days of changes
- Maintained electronically on BRS

## Company Maintenance

### 1. Change of Company Name

**Form**: CR6 (Change of Name)

**Requirements**:
- Special resolution by shareholders (75% approval)
- New name availability check
- Board resolution

**Fee**: **KES 1,050**

**Processing**: 3-5 business days

**Documents Issued**: New Certificate of Incorporation

### 2. Change of Directors

**Adding Director - Form CR2**:
- Director's consent
- ID/Passport copy
- KRA PIN
- **Fee**: **KES 650**

**Removing Director - Form CR8**:
- Board resolution or director's resignation
- Effective date
- **Fee**: **KES 650**

**Deadline**: Within 14 days of change (2026 14-Day Rule)

### 3. Change of Registered Office

**Form**: CR15 (Change of Address)

**Requirements**:
- New address details
- Board resolution
- Proof of address (lease/ownership)

**Fee**: **KES 650**

**Deadline**: Within 14 days of change

### 4. Change of Share Capital

**Increase Share Capital - Form CR16**:
- Special resolution
- New share capital amount
- **Fee**: Based on new capital amount

**Transfer of Shares - Form CR9**:
- Share transfer deed
- Transferor and transferee details
- Number of shares and consideration
- **Fee**: **KES 650**

**Deadline**: Within 30 days of transfer

### 5. Change of Company Secretary

**Form**: CR12A (Appointment/Removal of Secretary)

**Fee**: **KES 650**

**Note**: Company secretary is optional for private companies

## Document Retrieval

### 1. CR12 (Certificate of Good Standing)

**What is CR12?**
Certificate confirming company is registered and up-to-date with filings.

**Cost**: **KES 650**

**Processing**: Instant (if compliant) or 1-2 days

**Validity**: 3 months from issue date

**Requirements for Issuance**:
- All annual returns filed
- No pending compliance issues
- Company not struck off

**Uses**:
- Bank account opening
- Tender applications
- Loan applications
- Business partnerships
- Government contracts

### 2. Certificate of Incorporation

**Cost**: **KES 650** (certified copy)

**Processing**: 1-2 business days

**Uses**:
- Proof of company existence
- Opening bank accounts
- Registration with other authorities

### 3. Memorandum and Articles of Association

**Cost**: **KES 650** (certified copy)

**Processing**: 1-2 business days

### 4. BO E-Register (Beneficial Ownership Register)

**Cost**: **Free** (public access)

**Access**: Through BRS portal

**Information Available**:
- Names of beneficial owners
- Percentage of ownership
- Date of becoming beneficial owner
- Nature of control

### 5. Company Search

**Cost**: **KES 200**

**Information Provided**:
- Company name and registration number
- Date of incorporation
- Registered office
- Directors and shareholders
- Share capital
- Filing status

## Business Linking

### What is Business Linking?

Process of linking pre-2016 companies to the new BRS Kenya system.

### Who Needs to Link?

Companies incorporated before 2016 that are not yet on the BRS portal.

### How to Link Your Business

1. Visit BRS Kenya portal
2. Click "Link My Business"
3. Enter company registration number
4. Provide company details:
   - Company name
   - Date of incorporation
   - Directors' details
   - Registered office
5. Upload supporting documents:
   - Certificate of Incorporation
   - Latest CR12 (if available)
   - Directors' IDs
6. Submit for verification
7. Await approval (3-5 business days)
8. Receive login credentials

### After Linking

- File all outstanding annual returns
- Update beneficial ownership information
- Update directors and shareholders
- Obtain new CR12

## Common Questions

### Q1: How do I register a company on BRS Kenya?

**A**: Follow these steps:
1. Create account on https://brs.go.ke
2. Reserve company name (KES 100)
3. Complete CR1 form with:
   - Directors' details (ID, KRA PIN, contacts)
   - Shareholders' details
   - Share capital
   - Registered office address
4. Pay registration fee (KES 10,100 - 13,100 based on capital)
5. Submit application
6. Receive Certificate of Incorporation (1-3 days)
7. File BOF1 within 30 days

### Q2: When is my annual return due?

**A**: Your CR29 is due on your company's incorporation anniversary. For example, if incorporated on 20th June 2023, your annual return is due by 20th June 2024. Fee is KES 1,000, with KES 500 penalty per year if late.

### Q3: What happens if I don't file annual returns?

**A**: Consequences include:
- KES 500 penalty per year of delay
- Cannot obtain CR12 (Certificate of Good Standing)
- Company may be struck off register
- Directors may be personally liable
- Cannot participate in tenders
- Bank accounts may be frozen

### Q4: Who is a beneficial owner?

**A**: A beneficial owner is any individual who:
- Owns 10% or more of company shares
- Has 10% or more voting rights
- Can appoint/remove directors
- Exercises significant control over the company

Must be declared on BOF1 within 30 days. Penalty for non-compliance: KES 500,000.

### Q5: How much does CR12 cost?

**A**: CR12 (Certificate of Good Standing) costs **KES 650**. It's issued instantly if your company is compliant (all annual returns filed, no pending issues). Valid for 3 months.

### Q6: How do I add a new director?

**A**: To add a director:
1. Log in to BRS Kenya
2. Select your company
3. Navigate to "Directors"
4. Click "Add Director"
5. Complete CR2 form with director's details:
   - Full name
   - ID/Passport number
   - KRA PIN
   - Email and phone
   - Residential address
6. Upload director's ID copy
7. Pay KES 650
8. Submit (must be done within 14 days of appointment)

### Q7: What is the 14-Day Rule?

**A**: Starting 2026, all company changes must be filed within 14 days of occurrence:
- Director appointments/resignations
- Address changes
- Beneficial ownership changes
- Share transfers

Automatic penalties apply after 14 days.

### Q8: Can I file annual returns for multiple years at once?

**A**: Yes, you can file multiple years' annual returns. You'll pay:
- KES 1,000 per year (filing fee)
- KES 500 per year of delay (penalty)

Example for 3 years: (KES 1,000 × 3) + (KES 500 × 2) = KES 4,000

### Q9: How do I link my pre-2016 company?

**A**: Use the "Link My Business" feature on BRS portal:
1. Enter company registration number
2. Provide company details
3. Upload Certificate of Incorporation
4. Upload directors' IDs
5. Submit for verification
6. Await approval (3-5 days)
7. File all outstanding annual returns

### Q10: What documents do I need to open a company bank account?

**A**: Typically required:
- CR12 (Certificate of Good Standing) - KES 650
- Certificate of Incorporation
- Memorandum and Articles of Association
- Directors' IDs and KRA PINs
- Board resolution to open account
- Beneficial ownership information

### Q11: How long does it take to get a CR12?

**A**: If your company is compliant (all annual returns filed), CR12 is issued **instantly** in 2026. If not compliant, you must first file outstanding returns, then CR12 is issued within 1-2 days.

### Q12: Can a foreigner be a director of a Kenyan company?

**A**: Yes, foreigners can be directors. Requirements:
- Valid passport
- KRA PIN (can be obtained online)
- Email and phone contact
- Residential address

At least one director should be resident in Kenya for practical purposes.

### Q13: What is the difference between a shareholder and a director?

**A**:
- **Shareholder**: Owns shares in the company, entitled to dividends, votes at AGM
- **Director**: Manages day-to-day operations, makes business decisions, signs documents

One person can be both shareholder and director.

### Q14: How do I change my company name?

**A**: To change company name:
1. Check new name availability on BRS
2. Pass special resolution (75% shareholder approval)
3. Complete CR6 form
4. Upload board resolution
5. Pay KES 1,050
6. Submit application
7. Receive new Certificate of Incorporation (3-5 days)

### Q15: Is beneficial ownership information public?

**A**: Yes, the BO E-Register is publicly accessible through BRS portal. Anyone can search and view:
- Names of beneficial owners
- Percentage of ownership
- Nature of control

This promotes transparency and combats money laundering.
